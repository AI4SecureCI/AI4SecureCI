Key proposal and funding-related information for AI4SecureCI.

AI4SecureCI proposal details

  • Project name: AI4SecureCI.
  • Proposal information shown in the document: Submitted/PI: Dr. Maanak Gupta;
  • Project type: Collaborative multi-university CyberTraining Implementation proposal.
  • Participating institutions: Tennessee Technological University, North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University, and Rutgers University-Camden.
  • Primary focus: Training a research workforce in fairness-aware, AI-driven cloud cyberinfrastructure cybersecurity.
  • Core technical areas: Cloud cyberinfrastructure, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, machine learning, fairness, ethics, adversarial robustness, malware analysis, and explainable AI.

Project goals

  • Develop self-contained, adaptable, and extensible AI4SecureCI curriculum modules for undergraduate and graduate courses.
  • Create instructional materials such as lecture notes, hands-on labs, assignments, slides, study materials, and datasets.
  • Integrate AI4SecureCI modules into existing cloud computing, AI/ML, cybersecurity, and related courses.
  • Train a diverse cloud cyberinfrastructure research workforce through student boot camps, faculty workshops, tutorials, and mentored research experiences.
  • Support broad adoption of advanced cyberinfrastructure skills through public dissemination of modules, tools, datasets, and code repositories.

Training and dissemination

  • The project plans to provide online student boot camps, faculty mini-workshops, in-person faculty workshops, conference tutorials, and research-focused training activities.
  • The proposal emphasizes reaching diverse and underrepresented student communities, including students served by TTU, NCAT, and Rutgers University-Camden.
  • The project aims to make materials available through the project website, public repositories, CLARK/CARD, NSF ACCESS Knowledge Base, and GitHub.
  • The proposal also highlights the creation of AI4SecureCI ambassadors who can help disseminate the modules and support adoption at other institutions.

Project background mentioned in the document

  • Prior project impact described: Development of AI and cybersecurity modules, workshops, and training activities impacting diverse student groups.
