AI4SecureCI

Advancing fair, ethical, and AI-driven cybersecurity education for secure cloud cyberinfrastructure.

Project Introduction

AI4SecureCI is a collaborative CyberTraining project designed to prepare the next generation of researchers, students, and educators for the rapidly evolving challenges of cloud cyberinfrastructure security. As organizations increasingly depend on cloud services, virtualized systems, APIs, containers, and AI-driven technologies, the need for a skilled workforce capable of securing these environments has become more urgent than ever.

The project focuses on the intersection of cloud cyberinfrastructure, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, machine learning, fairness, ethics, and explainable AI. Through hands-on curriculum modules, practical labs, student boot camps, faculty workshops, and research-driven training activities, AI4SecureCI aims to make advanced cloud security education accessible, practical, and adaptable for undergraduate and graduate courses.

A central goal of AI4SecureCI is to broaden participation in advanced cyberinfrastructure research by engaging diverse and underrepresented student communities. The project brings together Tennessee Tech University, North Carolina A&T State University, and Rutgers University–Camden to develop and disseminate self-contained learning modules that help students build practical skills in secure cloud configuration, network security, automated access control, malware detection and classification, adversarial attacks and defenses, fairness-aware AI, and explainable AI for cloud security.

By combining technical training with ethical awareness, critical thinking, and research mentorship, AI4SecureCI seeks to build a trustworthy, fair, and resilient cloud cybersecurity workforce prepared to defend modern cyberinfrastructure against emerging threats.

Mission

AI4SecureCI develops self-contained, research-driven educational resources that help students and faculty understand, build, evaluate, and improve AI-based security solutions for cloud environments. The project emphasizes not only technical capability, but also fairness, explainability, responsible AI use, and awareness of how bias can affect cybersecurity systems.

Learning Experience

The curriculum is designed around experiential learning. Students work with cloud platforms, virtual machines, security monitoring tools, datasets, and machine learning libraries to study real security problems in cloud cyberinfrastructure. The modules support both conceptual understanding and hands-on practice, allowing learners to move from fundamentals to advanced topics such as online malware detection, malware family classification, adversarial learning, bias mitigation, and explainable machine learning.

These materials are intended to be flexible and pluggable so that instructors can integrate them into existing courses in cloud computing, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, machine learning, data mining, network security, and related areas without requiring students to take additional courses or delay graduation.

Community and Impact

AI4SecureCI supports a broader community through student boot camps, faculty workshops, tutorials, mentoring, course integration, and public dissemination of educational resources. By training students, supporting faculty adoption, and encouraging research participation, the project aims to expand access to advanced cyberinfrastructure skills and strengthen the national workforce needed to secure modern cloud systems.

The project especially emphasizes participation from diverse and underrepresented groups, helping create pathways for students to enter advanced cybersecurity and cloud cyberinfrastructure research. Through this combination of education, outreach, and research-based training, AI4SecureCI contributes to a more secure, trustworthy, and inclusive cyber future.
